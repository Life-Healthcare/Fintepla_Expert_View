NICE PUBLISHES TECHNOLOGY APPRAISAL GUIDANCE IN DRAVET SYNDROME

Dear ,

We are delighted to inform you that the National Institute for Health and Care Excellence (NICE) has published its technology appraisal guidance (TAG) for Fintepla(fenfluramine*) for the treatment of seizures associated with Dravet syndrome.

In the TAG, NICE recommends fenfluramine as an add-on to other antiepileptic medicines for treating seizures associated with Dravet syndrome in people aged 2 years and older, only if1:

  • seizures have not been controlled after trying 2 or more antiseizure medicines
  • the frequency of convulsive seizures is checked every 6 months, and fenfluramine is stopped if it has not fallen by at least 30% compared with the 6 months before starting treatment
  • the company provides fenfluramine according to the commercial arrangement.

View the full TAG here

Following the TAG publication on 8th July 2022, the NHS has now entered a 90-day implementation phase. This period enables the Trusts included in the Bluteq list (who will become prescribing centres) to prepare for when central funding becomes available for Fintepla. We anticipate that Day 91 will be 6th October 2022 when clinicians will be able to prescribe Fintepla to their patients for the treatment of seizures associated with Dravet syndrome, as described in the TAG guidance.

As part of the approval of Fintepla, there is a requirement for a Risk Management Programme (RMP) to ensure that the currently approved indication is strictly adhered to, and that physicians are adequately informed of the requirement to perform periodic echocardiogram monitoring when prescribing Fintepla.

View the RMP guidance here
